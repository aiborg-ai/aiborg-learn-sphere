## ğŸ§  What is Spaced Repetition?

Spaced repetition is a learning technique that presents information at increasing intervals over time. Instead of cramming, you review material just as you're about to forget it, leading to:

- **25% better retention** compared to traditional studying
- **Faster learning** with less total study time
- **Long-term memory formation** that lasts years, not days

Our system uses the **SM-2 algorithm** (the same one used by Anki), which has been scientifically proven to optimize learning efficiency.

### How It Works

1. **New cards** are reviewed after 1 day
2. If you remember, the next review is after **6 days**
3. Each successful review **doubles or triples** the interval
4. Cards you struggle with are shown **more frequently**
5. Easy cards appear **less often**, saving you time

## ğŸ’¯ Understanding Review Quality

The quality of your rating dramatically affects your learning efficiency.

### When to Press "Again" (1)

- You had **no idea** what the answer was
- You confused it with something else
- You got it **completely wrong**
- You want to see this card **again today**

**Effect:** Resets the card, you'll see it very soon

### When to Press "Hard" (2)

- You remembered but it took **significant effort**
- You had to **think for 10+ seconds**
- You got it partially right
- You want to see this card **more often**

**Effect:** Slight interval increase, card is marked as difficult

### When to Press "Good" (3)

- You remembered with **minor hesitation** (2-5 seconds)
- You got it **correct**
- This is your **default choice** for most reviews
- You want the **normal** review schedule

**Effect:** Standard interval increase (algorithm default)

### When to Press "Easy" (4)

- **Instant recall**, no thinking required
- You knew it **before seeing the back**
- The card feels **too easy** for you
- You want to see it **much later**

**Effect:** Maximum interval increase, card scheduled far in future

### âš ï¸ Common Mistakes

âŒ **Being too harsh** - Using "Again" when "Hard" is appropriate
- Result: You waste time reviewing cards you actually know

âŒ **Being too generous** - Using "Easy" when you hesitated
- Result: Cards come back too late and you forget

âœ… **Best approach**:
- Use "Good" (3) for 70% of cards
- Use "Again" only when genuinely stuck
- Save "Easy" for cards that feel trivial

### Creating Effective Flashcards

**The 20 Rules of Knowledge Formulation:**

1. **Understand before memorizing**
   - Don't create cards for concepts you don't understand
   - Read/learn the material first, then create cards

2. **Use the minimum information principle**
   - Simplest question, simplest answer
   - One fact per card

3. **Use cloze deletion for lists**
   - Front: "The three branches of US government are: [...]"
   - Back: "Executive, Legislative, Judicial"

4. **Avoid enumeration**
   - Instead of "List 5 types of...", create 5 separate cards

5. **Combat interference**
   - Don't create similar cards that confuse each other
   - Example: Don't make cards for "French 'hello'" and "Spanish 'hello'" in the same deck

6. **Optimize wording**
   - Use clear, unambiguous language
   - Avoid pronouns (he, she, it, they)

7. **Use images when helpful**
   - Visual learners benefit from diagrams
   - Flags, maps, charts, diagrams

8. **Provide context**
   - Include enough info to trigger recall
   - "In Python, how do you..." not "How do you..."

## â“ FAQ

### General Questions

**Q: How many cards should I study per day?**
A: Start with 20-30 new cards per day. Experienced users can handle 50-100. Quality over quantity!

**Q: What happens if I miss a day?**
A: Your streak resets, but your card progress is saved. Just continue reviewing the next day.

**Q: Can I share my decks with others?**
A: Public deck sharing is coming soon! Currently, decks are private.

**Q: How long until I see results?**
A: You'll notice improved retention within 2-3 weeks of consistent daily review.

**Q: What if I have too many due cards?**
A: Review what you can, the rest will remain due. Consider reducing new cards per day.

### Technical Questions

**Q: What algorithm does this use?**
A: We use the SM-2 algorithm, the same proven algorithm used by Anki (the most popular flashcard app).

**Q: How are intervals calculated?**
A:
- First review: 1 day
- Second review: 6 days
- Subsequent: previous interval Ã— easiness factor
- Failed reviews reset to 1 day

**Q: What's the "easiness factor"?**
A: A number (1.3-2.5+) that represents how easy a card is for you. It increases when you rate "Easy" and decreases with "Again".

**Q: Can I export my cards?**
A: Export functionality is planned. For now, your cards are stored securely in our database.

**Q: Do flashcards work offline?**
A: With our PWA (Progressive Web App), you can review cards offline! Install the app from your browser.

### Troubleshooting

**Q: I don't see any cards to review**
A: No cards are due! Come back later when cards are scheduled for review. Add more cards if needed.

**Q: A card keeps appearing even though I rate it "Easy"**
A: If you previously struggled with the card, it may need several "Easy" ratings to extend the interval significantly.

**Q: How do I delete a card?**
A: Go to the deck, find the card, and click the trash icon. Be careful - this is permanent!

**Q: The keyboard shortcuts aren't working**
A: Make sure you're not typing in a text field. Click on the flashcard area first.

**Q: My streak says 0 but I reviewed yesterday**
A: Check your timezone settings. Streaks are based on calendar days in your timezone.
